Closing Soon: Gordon Parks in Pittsburgh, 1944/1946
 
Final Day is Sunday, August 7, 2022
 
“How can it be that America’s greatest photographer produced a striking, original and insightful body of work highlighting an important aspect of the most dramatic event in the nation’s history and those pictures are almost unknown?” —Forbes
 
Gordon Parks in Pittsburgh, 1944/1946 features more than 50 never-before-seen photographs taken by legendary photojournalist Gordon Parks while on assignment in Pittsburgh. Parks’s photographs taken at the Penola, Inc. grease plant endure as a record of humanity and everyday life in the mid-20th century, telling countless stories that have been overlooked.

Date with a Docent
 
Thursday, 6, 6:30, and 7 p.m.
Saturday, 10:30 and 11 a.m.
Free with admission, registration required
 
Pittsburgh’s Strip District of the 1940s with one of our esteemed docents before Gordon Parks in Pittsburgh, 1944/1946 closes!
 
A Date with a Docent is a one-on-one or small-group personalized mini-tour that offers visitors an in-depth look at a specific area of the museum’s collection, exhibitions, or architecture.
 
This Thursday and Saturday, join us for a Date with a Docent during the final days of Gordon Parks in Pittsburgh, 1944/1946 and explore Gordon Parks’s visual record of the Penola, Inc. grease plant, its workers, and their activities manufacturing lubricants to support US military efforts during World War II.
